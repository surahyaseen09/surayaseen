Surah Al-Fatir comes before Surah Yaseen, and Surah As-Saffat comes after it in the Quran. This Surah has 83 verses, 733 words, and 3,068 letters, divided into 5 sections (Rukus). Surah Yaseen highlights the core teachings of Islam in a clear and simple way. It emphasizes belief in one Allah and rejects all forms of idol worship. The Surah also speaks about the Day of Judgment, where believers will be rewarded and disbelievers will face consequences. It reminds people about the afterlife and the importance of faith.

Core Message Of Surah Yaseen

Here are three important lessons from Surah Yaseen, derived from the Quran, Hadith, and teachings of verified Islamic scholars:

1. The Oneness of Allah (Tawhid) and Rejection of Shirk: Surah Yaseen strongly emphasizes the absolute Oneness of Allah (Tawhid). Verses like, “And a sign for them is the dead land: We give it life, and We bring forth from it grain, so that they eat thereof.” (36:33) and “Is it not He Who created the heavens and the earth, and is not able to create the like of them? Yes, indeed! And He is the All-Knowing Creator.” (36:81) highlight Allah’s power and ability to create and sustain all things. This underscores the importance of worshipping Allah alone and rejecting any form of associating partners with Him (Shirk). This is a foundational lesson, as it is the core of Islam.

2. The Reality of the Afterlife (Akhirah), Resurrection, and Accountability: Surah Yaseen provides detailed descriptions of the events of the Day of Judgement, the resurrection, and the consequences of actions in this life. Verses such as “And the Horn will be blown, and at once from the graves, unto their Lord, they will hasten.” (36:51) and the narratives of the people of the city (36:13-29) serve as warnings and reminders about the importance of preparing for the afterlife. Scholars emphasize the significance of this lesson in motivating believers to live righteous lives and strive for good deeds. The Surah reminds that all will be held accountable for their deeds.

3. The Importance of Prophethood and Following Divine Guidance: The Surah presents stories of prophets and their encounters with their people. The story of the people of the city, who rejected the messengers sent to them, serves as a cautionary tale. The Surah implicitly highlights the necessity of believing in and following the guidance brought by the Prophets (peace be upon them all) and adhering to Allah‚Äôs commands. The Surah’s emphasis on the consequences of rejecting divine guidance underscores the importance of heeding the message of Islam.
